About Me

Want to know more about me? Let me take you on a little journey about my education and professional experience so far. I promise you it will not take long!...

It all started with a degree in Management Informatics obtained in Universidade Lusófona de Humanidades e Tecnologias (Lisbon). The objective for me at the time with this degree was to get a broad view on the connection between the computer science world and the management of the companies. Because of this, the degree had a nice balance between the technical computer science subjects (like programming languages, data bases, operating systems, etc.) and the management subjects (like accounting, management tools, etc.). It was also with this degree that I had my first connection with the Web Development world, doing an End of Course project on how we could use a CMS software and tweek it to give more accessibility on websites to handicapped people.

After this degree, I was starving to have my first professional work experience! So I entered in a 1 year internship at Grupo Bertrand Círculo (Lisbon). This company is known in Portugal for the Bertrand bookstores placed all over the country, with one of them being the oldest bookstore in Portugal, located in Chiado, Lisbon. My role there as an intern was to investigate and test a method to help the bookstore employees on better understanding new developments lauched in the softwares in wich they work daily, or just to help the recently hired persons to have a platform where they could learn the basics of their job first. In general, Bertrand wanted an interactive E-Learning platform for their workers. So my job was to first investigate some E-Learning software solutions on the market, test them, and decide with the company the best one to buy. Then, I went to the bookstores to gather and understand the main activities of the employees, and draw this processes in simple workflows.

From here on, the fun part started and the challenge was to build interactive E-Learning courses for the employees! The main process of an E-Learning course wanted by the company was to first record a vídeo with an experienced person on the subject it was being teached, and then build a simulation of the teached process for the people to test what they leaned. In the end of the internship, I passed all my know how to the company so they could continue the work on the E-Learning platform, as this was mainly an individual project. This experience was very fun, and it was a good way to start understanding how a medium sized company works.

Finishing the internship, I received the oportunity to enter in a small IT consultant company called Teksajo, which worked closely with Grupo Bertrand Circulo. Their job was do develop new functionalities in the 2 main softwares used by Bertrand: the “front store” application used in the bookstores to do all the selling operations with the clients, and the “back store” application used in the bookstores and headquarters of Bertrand to do the back-end operations, like accounting and orders processing to the publishing companies. My role in Teksajo was to work on the development and support of this 2 softwares used by Bertrand.

In the “front store” I had a more functional approach, doing the requirements gathering and construction of functional documents with new developments to be made. I also had the role to test the received developments from the technical team, and to follow the user tests in Bertrand. By the other hand, on the “back store” software I had a more technical approach, doing a lot of technical development and coding. This software was based in the IBM AS/400 system, and the programing language used to code there was RPG. Getting the hang of the RPG language was a real challenge, as this was an old programming language that is not teached anymore in schools! I stayed in Teksajo for 2 years, and this was by far the place where I learned more technically and functionally.

After Teksajo, I entered in a new project on a diferent IT consultant company called Agap2. This was a much bigger company from what I was used to, and the project was to work as a permanente consultant in one of the BNP Paribas bank facilities in Lisbon. My role there was to be part of a technical support team on the Wealth Management department.

The bank used the Olympic software on the AS/400 system for a lot of activities in the financial makets, so my know how on the AS/400 system was crucial for this work. The main acitivity of this support team was to guarantee the first and second line of support to the Olympic application for all the users of the bank using this software in Lisbon, Madrid, London, Jersey Island, Middle East, Geneve and Monaco. This support included the investigation of bugs and errors reported by the users, and also a release management role on new developments launched in Olympic. Because of this, we had to keep a close communication on all time with the AS/400 development team placed in Singapore, and with the implementation team and owners of the AS/400 machine in Geneve. We also had the responsibility to test new software fixes provided by Singapore, and support the user acceptance tests on big releases.

I stayed in this project for 1,5 years, and this time was crucial to help me understanding all the workflows and complexity of such a big company like the BNP Paribas bank. Also the challenge of supportting an application with so much users taught me a lot of valuable lessons on stress management situations.

It was towards the end of the BNP Paribas project that I started feeling a lack of motivation on the technologies I was working on, and wanted to do the shift to some of the cool things being done on the Web! To achieve this, I decided to leave the AS/400 behind and started a small journey to learn more on the Web Development world so I could then find an opportunity in this area.

I started by taking an intensive professional degree specialized in Web Development at FLAG (Lisbon), wich had a mix between front-end and back-end development. I learned the basics on HTML, CSS, JavaScript, MySQL, PHP, and had small workshops on JQuery and CMS (Wordpress). It were 132 hours in total, but all teached in 1 single month, 6 hours per day, so there was not much time to practice and consolidate all the information...

To solve this, I decided to take another professional degree, but this time more focused on the front-end development part that I felt in love with! So I took a slightly bigger professional degree on Front-End Development at EDIT (Lisbon). The curriculum on this degree was an introduction to UX/UI, and then a more in depth learning on HTML5, CSS3, SASS, Responsive, JavaScript, JQuery and a workshop on the React framework. We also had a big project where we had teams made with people from diferent courses to work on a real world company briefing, doing all the Branding Strategy, UX/UI and Front-End development. This degree were 245 hours in total, 12 hours per week, so it gave me more free time to practice.

In the available free time, I took the initiative to do some online courses and tutorials on FreeCodeCamp, CodeCademy, and of course, to do this little portfolio site you’re checking right now!

I also spent some of my time on this period volunteering myself in a shelter of abandoned animals to help them in everything I could. Nature and Animal Welfare are 2 very important causes for me, and giving a little help by my own brings me peace of mind and a great joy!

So there you have it, that’s all about me for now! Don’t hesitate to reach me down below!
